## Key Finding

All fixed-threshold industry approaches share CBD's structural failure on the
slow-burn scenario. At any threshold maintaining FPR ≤ 5%, the window-averaged
signal from `export_data` stays sub-threshold during the logistic ramp for
60%+ of trials. Methods that eventually fire do so 900-1000 actions post-
calibration (≈7 hours), vs. SS-CBD median 20 actions (9 min, 19× faster).

Only sequential SPRT-based methods (SS-CBD, LRT) accumulate evidence
across every step of the ramp, achieving 100% early detection.

## Structural Explanation

The logistic ramp ensures that `export_data`'s instantaneous rate at step t is a mixture p(t) = (1−σ(t))·0.5% + σ(t)·6.7%, where σ(t) ≤ 0.5 for the first 300 actions of the ramp (steps 500–800). In a 200-action window centred on this region, the expected count of `export_data` is ≈ 0.5·(0.5%+6.7%)·200 = 7.2 — only marginally above the baseline count of 1.0. Any threshold k ≥ 10 that keeps FPR ≤ 5% on control traces therefore misses the first half of the ramp in most trials. Sequential SPRT integrates the likelihood ratio continuously, detecting the fractional signal increase immediately.
